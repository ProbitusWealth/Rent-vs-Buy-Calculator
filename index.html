How to use this Calculator?

Deciding between renting or buying a home can be tough. Our calculator can help you weigh your options and make an informed decision.

1. Monthly Rent:

This is the amount you would pay each month to rent a similar property. Consider factors like location, property size, and amenities when estimating the rent.

2. Property Value:

This is the current market value of the property you are considering buying. You can check property listings online, consult with real estate agents, or use property valuation tools to estimate the value.

3. Down Payment (%):

The down payment is the initial amount you pay upfront when purchasing a property. A higher down payment can lead to lower monthly EMIs and better loan terms. Typical down payments range from 10% to 20% of the property value, but this can vary based on lender policies and individual circumstances.

4. EMI Rate (%):

The EMI rate is the interest rate charged by the lender on your home loan. This rate can fluctuate based on market conditions and your creditworthiness. You can get an estimate from banks or use online calculators to determine a potential EMI rate.

5. Tenure (Years):

The tenure is the duration of your home loan and also the number of years you plan to stay in the home. A longer tenure results in lower monthly EMIs but higher total interest paid over the loan term. A shorter tenure leads to higher EMIs but lower overall interest costs.

6. Rate of Appreciation (Property):

This is the estimated annual increase in the property's value over time. Historical data and market trends can be used to estimate this rate, but it's important to note that property values can fluctuate.

7. Expected Rate on Savings:

This is the estimated return you expect to earn on your savings if you choose to rent instead of buy. Consider factors like investment options, risk tolerance, and market conditions when estimating this rate.

Result:

Calculates Future Value of Savings: If you choose to rent, it calculates how much your savings would grow over time.
Calculates Future Value of Property: If you choose to buy, it estimates how much your property value will increase.

Outcome:

The calculator provides a recommendation based on the comparison. If the future value of your savings is significantly higher than the total cost of buying, it suggests renting, which might be more cost-effective. Otherwise, it suggests buying.
